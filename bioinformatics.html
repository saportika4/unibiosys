BIOINFORMATICS

Information gathering and knowledge discovery is the latest challenge in modern biology. Genomic information retrieval, better understanding of disease mechanism and novel drug discovery solutions has made possible through bioinformatics approaches. With the intention of providing high end services and quality education in Bioinformatics, UniBiosys Biotech Research Labs have formed bioinformatics research division, comprising computational biologists and bioinformatics scientists.

BIOINFORMATICS SERVICES

A wide range of research services are offered at UniBiosys for the pharmaceutical/ Biotech/ Life science industries.




MOLECULAR DESIGN AND DRUG DEVELOPMENT

In-silico drug design and development services are provided by UniBiosys as per the clients need from target identification, target validation, hit identification, hit to lead development, lead optimization, small molecule design, In-silico toxicology prediction, Binding affinity calculation etc. UBRL explore novel molecule identification as well as refinement of the existing molecules.

NEXT GENERATION SEQUENCE ANALYSIS

Large sized experimental genomic data obtained due to its complexity has become a major issue in many molecular biology labs. UniBiosys offers genome data analysis, SNP analysis, Mutation detection and assigning the functional and morphological classification based on the client's requirements.

DATA MINING & DATA ANALYSIS

We at UniBiosys are capable of mining the client specific biological data. Generally, data mining (sometimes called knowledge discovery) is the process of analyzing data from different perspectives and summarizing it into useful information that can be used to increase revenue, reduce costs, or both.

ALLIANCE AMONG INDUSTRY AND ACADEMICS

UniBiosys aims to create a platform for the promotion and collaboration in the field of Bioinformatics. UniBiosys associates with Companies, Universities, Colleges, Biological experts, Research Organizations and work closely for enhancing the opportunities in the fertile area of Bioinformatics.
